Where Tustin Arrests Are Processed

Someone arrested in Tustin is transported to the Tustin Police Department Jail at 300 Centennial Way, Tustin, CA 92780 (phone: 714-573-3200). The Tustin Police Department operates a Type I detention facility inside its headquarters at 300 Centennial Way. It handles initial booking for nearly all arrests made inside Tustin, including arrests out of Old Town, The District at Tustin Legacy, and the major retail corridors.

For serious felony charges or when the city facility is at capacity, defendants transfer to the Orange County Intake and Release Center (IRC) at 550 N Flower Street, Santa Ana, CA 92703. This is the intake point for the Orange County Sheriff's Department (OCSD) jail system, which also includes the Theo Lacy Facility in Orange and the James A. Musick Facility in Irvine. Angels Bail Bonds posts bonds at every one of these facilities.

Tustin in Detail — What Makes This City's Bail Cases Different

Tustin has its own police department and a city jail at 300 Centennial Way, right off the 5 freeway and adjacent to Tustin City Hall. TPD handles initial booking for all city arrests, and the jail is large enough to hold defendants for the first several hours before any transfer is considered. This matters because posting a bond at the TPD jail is usually the fastest path to release — faster than waiting for a transfer to the county system.

Geographically, Tustin is one of the busiest arrest zones in Central Orange County. The city is bordered by the 5, 55, and 261 freeways, with the old Tustin Marine Corps Air Station blimp hangars now at the center of a major residential and commercial redevelopment. DUI enforcement is heavy along the 5 freeway corridor and at the 55/22 interchange near The District shopping center. Tustin PD also runs aggressive retail-theft enforcement at The District and The Market Place — one of the most heavily patrolled commercial districts in the county.

Felonies and county-level holds transfer to the Theo Lacy Facility in Orange, a 10-minute drive north on the 55. The practical implication: if a bond is not posted within roughly 4 hours of booking at TPD, the defendant will likely be transferred, and release time doubles. Angels Bail Bonds confirms the bail amount at TPD before transfer and begins paperwork the moment booking is complete.

How a Tustin Bail Bond Actually Works

You call (626) 478-1062. We confirm the bail amount from the Orange County bail schedule — tustin bail bonds typically range from $5,000 for petty theft (PC 484) to $50,000 for domestic violence (PC 273.5) and $100,000 for DUI with injury (VC 23153). You pay Angels Bail Bonds a 10% premium — the state-set rate under California Insurance Code § 1800.4. We post the full bail amount with the jail as a surety bond. The defendant is released, typically 1–4 hours at Tustin PD Jail and 4–8 hours at the Orange County Intake and Release Center (IRC).

The 10% is a fee for services — it is a premium paid to the bondsman, not a loan. When the case concludes and all court appearances are made, the bond is exonerated.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where does a Tustin arrest actually get processed?

Tustin arrests are processed at the TPD Jail at 300 Centennial Way, right off the 5 freeway. Bonds posted here generally result in 1–3 hour release for misdemeanors. Felonies and overflow transfer to Theo Lacy Facility in Orange.

How much does a bail bond actually cost in Tustin?

The bail bond fee is 10% of the total bail amount under California Insurance Code § 1800.4. For a first-offense DUI with bail set at $15,000, you pay $1,500. That 10% is our fee for posting the full bond — it is not refunded at case conclusion. Payment plans are available for qualifying clients.

Can Tustin bail be posted the same night?

Yes, in most cases. California does not impose a mandatory overnight hold. Once the booking process is complete and the Orange County bail schedule amount is assigned, a licensed bondsman can post the bond immediately. Most Tustin DUI and misdemeanor arrests result in same-night or early-morning release.
