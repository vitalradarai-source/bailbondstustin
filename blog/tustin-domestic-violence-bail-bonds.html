Tustin Domestic Violence Bail Bonds

When Tustin Police Department responds to a domestic violence call and makes an arrest, the path forward depends on the severity of the charge. For felony domestic violence — most commonly under PC § 273.5 — the person arrested is transported to the Orange County Sheriff's Department Theo Lacy Facility at 501 The City Dr S, Orange, CA 92868 for booking and detention. Misdemeanor arrests under PC § 243(e)(1) may be processed locally through Tustin PD before a transfer is arranged, though county detention remains the standard for overnight holds. Regardless of where booking occurs, California law allows bail to be posted immediately once the process is complete — there is no mandatory waiting period before a bond can be placed.

Emergency Protective Orders and Bail in Tustin

Tustin PD officers responding to a domestic disturbance are required under California law to issue an Emergency Protective Order (EPO) when certain threshold conditions are met. The EPO takes effect at the moment it is served and typically remains valid for five court business days — after which the case moves to a criminal or family court for a longer-term order if sought. A critical point that many families misunderstand: an EPO is a contact restriction, not a detention order. It restricts who the arrested person can communicate with or be near — it does not prevent them from being released on bail. Bail can be posted and the person released while the EPO remains fully in force; they simply must comply with its terms or face a separate criminal violation.

If your family's situation involves shared housing or children in common, a defense attorney can appear at the first court date to request modifications that allow indirect contact through counsel or third parties, which is often necessary to address practical family matters without violating the order.

What a Bail Bond Costs Under California Law

The bail bond premium in California is fixed by state law at exactly 10% of the total bail amount under California Insurance Code § 1800.4. Every licensed bail agent in the state charges this same rate — there is no room to negotiate it higher or lower, and any agent offering a significantly lower rate should be viewed with suspicion. For a PC § 273.5 felony bail of $50,000, the premium is $5,000. For a bail of $100,000 on an enhanced felony, the premium is $10,000. For a misdemeanor PC § 243(e)(1) bail of $15,000, the premium is $1,500.
